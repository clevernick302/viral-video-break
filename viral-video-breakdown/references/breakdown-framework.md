# Viral Video Breakdown Framework

Use this reference when analyzing short-form videos and extracting reusable creative formulas.

## Core Equation

```text
Viral potential = topic demand x first-3-second stop power x retention structure x emotional intensity x propagation reason x remixability
```

## Viral Context Signals

Prioritize relative performance over raw views.

- Strong outlier: views are much higher than the creator's recent median.
- Small-account breakout: views greatly exceed follower count.
- Engagement quality: comments show debate, tagging friends, questions, identity claims, or intent to try.
- Save/share potential: practical templates, recipes, checklists, scripts, locations, product finds, or emotionally shareable ideas.

When saves, shares, retention, and account baseline are unavailable, say so.

## Hook Types

- Result first: show the outcome before explaining the process.
- Mistake warning: tell the viewer what to stop doing.
- Secret reveal: imply hidden knowledge or unfair advantage.
- Identity callout: name the exact audience.
- Before/after contrast: create an immediate gap.
- Conflict: person vs person, belief vs belief, expectation vs result.
- Challenge or experiment: promise a test with uncertain outcome.
- Curiosity object: show something visually unusual without explaining it immediately.
- Pain-point mirror: reflect a specific frustration.
- Speedrun value: promise a fast transformation or checklist.

## First 3 Seconds Rubric

Score each 0-2:

- First frame clarity: clear subject, no visual ambiguity.
- Motion or interruption: something changes immediately.
- Target relevance: the intended viewer recognizes themselves.
- Curiosity gap: a question is opened.
- Stakes: viewer senses gain, loss, surprise, status, money, beauty, skill, or belonging.

Interpretation:

- 0-3: weak hook.
- 4-6: functional hook.
- 7-10: strong hook with breakout potential.

## Retention Mechanisms

- Open loop: a question stays unresolved.
- Curiosity stacking: each answer opens a new question.
- Escalating proof: examples get stronger over time.
- Visual reset: new frame, angle, text, prop, or setting every 1-3 seconds.
- Pattern interrupt: unexpected sound, zoom, cut, pause, contradiction, or action.
- Delayed payoff: result shown later, not immediately.
- Progress ladder: step 1, step 2, step 3.
- Contrast chain: bad vs good, old vs new, cheap vs expensive, beginner vs expert.
- Emotional reversal: fear to relief, confusion to clarity, doubt to proof.
- Micro-cliffhanger: each beat ends before the viewer feels finished.

## Timeline Template

Use 1-3 second beats for short videos:

| Time | Visual | Audio/Text | Edit Move | Function | Viewer Psychology |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0-3s | First frame/action | First line/text | Cut/zoom/sound | Stop scroll | Curiosity/stakes |
| 3-10s | Setup | Promise/problem | Pace changes | Confirm relevance | "This is for me" |
| Middle | Examples/proof | Steps/story | Resets | Sustain attention | Progress/open loops |
| Ending | Payoff/CTA | Summary/twist | Final beat | Convert engagement | Save/share/rewatch |

## Emotional Triggers

- Curiosity: "I need to know what happens."
- Anxiety: "I might be doing this wrong."
- Aspiration: "I want that result."
- Relief: "Finally, a simple solution."
- Identity: "This is people like me."
- Conflict: "I need to pick a side."
- Humor: "This is surprising or socially shareable."
- Awe: "I did not know this was possible."
- Practical utility: "I should save this."
- Social currency: "Sharing this makes me useful or interesting."

## Engagement Diagnosis

Ask separately:

- Like: what moment created agreement, delight, or reward?
- Comment: what invites debate, confession, question, correction, or tagging?
- Save: what would the viewer need later?
- Share: who would the viewer send this to, and why?
- Rewatch: what was too fast, surprising, layered, or looped?

## Formula Extraction

Convert the video from specific to structural:

```text
Specific: "Creator shows a skincare mistake and fixes it with one product."
Formula: "Mistake warning -> consequence -> simple fix -> proof -> saveable rule."
```

Then adapt:

```text
For [niche], use [hook type] to expose [common mistake/desire], prove it with [example/proof], and end with [save/share reason].
```

## Final Compact Summary

Always try to produce one sentence:

```text
This video works because it uses [hook] to stop [viewer], creates [curiosity/tension], sustains attention through [retention mechanism], and triggers [engagement behavior].
```
